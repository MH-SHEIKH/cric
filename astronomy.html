Information

Stars form inside relatively dense concentrations of interstellar gas and dust known as molecular clouds. These regions are extremely cold (temperature about 10 to 20K, just above absolute zero). At these temperatures, gases become molecular meaning that atoms bind together. CO and H2 are the most common molecules in interstellar gas clouds. The deep cold also causes the gas to clump to high densities. When the density reaches a certain point, stars form.

The Milky Way is our home, a barred spiral galaxy about 100,000 light-years across, containing billions of stars and planets. From Earth, we see the Milky Way as a faint, milky band of light across the night sky, which is a view of the disk of stars from our vantage point within it.

About Us

Astronomy is the scientific study of celestial objects and phenomena beyond Earth's atmosphere, including planets, stars, galaxies, and comets. It uses physics, mathematics, and chemistry to understand their origin, evolution, and properties. Astronomy includes observational astronomy, which involves collecting data, and theoretical astronomy, which creates models to explain that data.
